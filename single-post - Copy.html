  • by Siya Percy
  • timeless read
  • 29 Comment

Lately I've been seeing a repeation in human behaviour and while I experience this, my mind tells me that I am special for learnig this but then my gut argues agaist it. It argues that if I see it, then everyone else see it too and many have taken their place in the repeatition. It is a must that one takes a place in the repeatition...That! or opening the 3rd eye. Opening the third eye is abstract...it is more than just thinking things, more like living in a state of your own imagination while clearly intune with the collective reality, the global conscience. the logic from the human experience...the rest of the world. It is undertanding what the bigger picture requires of you while everything that matters to you is your little reality. This, you will fight for. You will not let anyone question your judgement...

As you read this, you will wonder how true is this...it doesn't matter!!

What matters is the task at hand. What ever project you're working on right now...That's your little picture. Even if it will affect the whole world at some point...right now it is your little picture... See it...See you.... Open ALL your eyes... Wider, wider, Take a deep breath, Hold it, Hold it, Breath... Look at the painting, Find yourself....

You want to be where your spots are on black and White. If you are on the vibrating spots you are distracted. If you are on the maze, you need to meditate... If you gravitate to wards the two red spots, you are blind and that is danger.

Ingredients
  • Ingredients
  • 1 cup (240 ml) water, plus more as needed
  • 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3/4 cup (120 g) fine polenta
  • 3 cups sunflower oil, plus more as needed
  • 7 ounces (200 g) peeled parsnips, very thinly sliced on a mandoline
  • 1 pinch fine sea salt, plus more to taste
  • 2 tablespoons (30 g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 tablespoon maple syrup (up to 1 tablespoon as needed)
Directions
  • 1. Combine all of the ingredients, kneading to form a smooth dough.
  • 2. Allow the dough to rise, in a lightly greased, covered bowl, until it’s doubled in size, about 90 minutes.
  • 3. Gently divide the dough in half; it’ll deflate somewhat.
  • 4. Gently shape the dough into two oval loaves; or, for longer loaves, two 10″ to 11″ logs. Place the loaves on a lightly greased or parchment-lined baking sheet. Cover and let rise until very puffy, about 1 hour. Towards the
  • 5. end of the rising time, preheat the oven to 425°F.
  • 6. Spray the loaves with lukewarm water.
  • 7. Make two fairly deep diagonal slashes in each; a serrated bread knife, wielded firmly, works well here.
  • 8. Bake the bread for 25 to 30 minutes, until it’s a very deep golden brown. Remove it from the oven, and cool on a rack.
